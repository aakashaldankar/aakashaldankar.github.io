The birds were already at it when I opened my eyes.

I didn't know what kind. Had to google it. Something melodious, I wrote in the search bar. The results didn't matter. I stayed at the window anyway, watching Bangalore wake up slowly, light spreading across the rooftops of big bungalows, a few fancy cars sitting still in driveways like they owned the silence.

I've been sleeping without a mattress for a week now.

Not because I can't afford one. I ordered it. It arrives next week. Until then, it's just me and the wooden frame and the understanding that you don't always get to have everything at once.

It's fine. Surprisingly fine.

---

It's been a little over a month in this city. The first few weeks I was in a PG, single occupancy, rules on the wall, a bit far from work, a ceiling that didn't feel like mine. So I looked. One week of real estate hunts, WhatsApp forwards from property owners, auto rides to their addresses. Then I found this place.

Not a building. A bungalow.

The owner lives below. I live on top. Quiet area. The kind of quiet where you notice things, the velvet beige curtains I hung on my big windows, a water purifier humming in the corner, and the egg boiler that's doing more work than it probably expected.

Market is five minutes on foot.

I am, slowly, setting up a life here.

---

Saturday had no plan. That was the plan.

Freshened up, had breakfast, worked out for an hour, first time in a while. The kind of workout where your body is surprised you showed up. Skimmed through old notes I'd made to myself. Things I want to do, projects I keep meaning to start very soon.

Then talked to one of my closest friends, he is in Germany.

We talked for nearly two hours. Life updates, his, mine, the general chaos of our mid-twenties. We talked about AI like two people who find it genuinely interesting and slightly terrifying. Made travel plans we'll probably postpone twice before we actually go. It was good. The kind of call that makes you realise you've been meaning to make it for too long.

---

After that, the mattress-less bed again. For a nap.

I don't usually sleep in the afternoon. But there was nothing urgent. So I lay down, on the wood and the bedsheet, and I went under for an hour.

Woke up, watched some YouTube. Thought about being productive. Opened a blank document and wrote about AI projects I want to build. Got a few paragraphs in. Closed the laptop.

---

6:30 and the light was going golden.

I put on youtube, walked out, scouted the streets around the bungalow, the big houses, the parked cars that don't move much, a market that smells like vegetables and fruits and something sweet I haven't identified yet. Picked up dinner on the way back. South Indian. Something I hadn't tried before.

I've been doing that, eating something new each day. The restaurants nearby are good. Healthy, mostly. This city knows how to feed you.

Talked with family after dinner. Watched a machine learning lecture on YouTube. Read a few blogs about recent AI things. Got interested. Then got bored.

Then thought: I should write something.

Which is how I end up here.

---

I want to start going to events in this city. Bangalore has them, just like Mumbai, startup meetups, tech exhibitions, the kind of rooms where founders and product managers and investors are all in the same space pretending to be casual about it. Soon.

But today was not that kind of day. And I like those too, the ones with no agenda, no outcome, just you moving through the hours.

Some days the point is just to be alive in them.

---

Before I sleep tonight, I'm going up to the terrace.

The bungalow has one. I haven't used it enough.

I want to stand up there and watch the city go to sleep, lights going out in windows one by one, the street sounds fading, the silence getting louder in that way it does late at night when you stop talking over it.

And then I'll come back down.

Lie on the mattress-less bed.

It arrives next week. But tonight, this is enough.
