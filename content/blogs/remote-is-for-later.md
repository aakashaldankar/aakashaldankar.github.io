Jan 22nd. Last day at the startup.

I signed off, handed back the laptop, hugged everyone. Then somehow we all started talking about AI projects again. One last time. That's the thing about people who love their work, goodbyes turn into meetings.

I met our co-founder. Thanked him. Promised to be available if anything came up on the projects I'd owned.

Then I sat at my usual desk. Took some photos.

Two friends walked me out. Promised to stay connected. Meant it.

Good people. Good day.

---

But I need to go back a bit.

Because Jan 22nd didn't arrive suddenly. It arrived slowly, then all at once.

---

My first day was June 2025.

I came at 9am for an 11am call. Ha!

The office was nearly empty. Three people were there, heads down, working on something. They were friendly. Introduced me to their projects. Started fielding my questions.

Hundreds of questions. I could not stop.

Later, those same three people were told: you're doing his interview. C'mon.

I made it through. Then two more interview rounds with the founder, then the co-founder. Two days after that, I was in. AI Engineer. Officially.

---

My first project was an automated financial planner system.

Huge codebase. Took me a week just to understand what was happening in those files.

A month later, the rest of the team had been moved to other projects. Startup life. You wear every hat there is. Solo dev one week, team player the next, sometimes taking on interviews of freshers.

The financial planner was mine now. Entirely.

I loved it.

Weekly client calls. I would demo progress, take feedback, discuss what came next. Full creative control. The kind of ownership fresh engineers rarely get this early.

---

Life found a rhythm.

Tea breaks in the morning. Walks after lunch. Another tea by evening. WFH when I needed to prepare for a client call.

The minus?

10:30pm calls. The BDA person would ping: new client pitch tonight, you're joining.

Most people call that overwork. I called it free business school. I'd sit in and watch the pitch unfold. How to propose. How to talk. How to close. I was there to answer technical questions. But I was absorbing everything else.

You can learn anywhere if you choose to.

---

I built a routine.

6:20am, alarm.

Ready by 7:15, out the door.

Office by 8:20am.

The rest of the team rolled in around 11. Startup perks. They worked from home first, came in later. Because I arrived early, I could leave early. 4:30pm, sometimes 5:30. Home around 7:30. Time with family. Then a personal project. Then sleep.

Do it again.

---

Weekends were mine.

Workouts in the morning. Reading business books when I felt like skipping the gym. Writing ideas on pages and filing them in a drawer for later. The drawer was getting full.

My notes were meticulous. Workout plans. Study plans. When I showed my gym instructor my workout notes, the breakdowns, the sets, the progressions, he paused.

Then asked me to make one for him too. Ha!

Evenings, my friend would come on his motorcycle. We'd ride to Juhu beach. Sit there and just talk. About everything going on in each other's lives. Sometimes snacks on the sand.

After a point we used to say: we really came on a date with each other.

That's Juhu in the evenings. Good light. Good conversation. Good company.

Also I'd read on weekends. Business books mostly. Vocabulary. English. My spoken English isn't good. I know it. That's one reason I write. I've always liked writing. Ideas on pages. Pages in a drawer. For later. This blog honestly I'll refine it iteratively, put it through an LLM before it goes up. Refine the words a bit. But the goal is to not need LLM for this someday. I'm not in a hurry.

---

But something was shifting inside.

Quietly at first. Then louder.

I'd been upskilling continuously. Understanding market needs. Learning new things. Getting real exposure to clients, product, code, pitches. And slowly a question started forming.

What if I aimed higher?

Big companies. Better scale. More challenge.

The void wasn't unhappiness. The startup was good to me. The void was ambition quietly asking: is this enough?

By December I had my answer. No.

---

December. 6:20am alarm. Same as always.

But before anyone else arrived, I'd open LeetCode.

Coding problems. ML/DL concept revision. YouTube tutorials. The morning hours were mine and I used every minute.

By 11am I'd start my actual job. By 7:30pm I'd leave. Home by 9:30pm.

Some people would call that grinding. I call it wanting something badly enough.

I am just like this. When I want something, I put everything I have into it. I imagine the goal. I work toward it. Nothing else gets the energy.

---

The calls started coming.

Good companies. Technical rounds. Some I cleared. Some I didn't.

Failed a few coding rounds early on. Kept going. Had to juggle project assignments from one company while another's interview was scheduled the same week. Current job running in the background the whole time.

It was a lot.

But I had two parallel processes running by the end.

One: a remote Data Scientist role. Fully work-from-home. Six interview stages because if you're hiring remotely you better be sure. I made it through five rounds. Round six was time series. Statistical models. ARIMA, SARIMA, the whole family.

I hadn't worked much on those. Couldn't answer the questions the way they needed.

Two days later, an email arrived. Polite language. "We've decided to go with candidates at a later stage of the process."

A good way to say no. I appreciated it.

I wanted that role. But things don't always go the way you plan. Onwards.

---

The Bangalore process was still running.

AI/ML Engineer. Hybrid. Technical rounds, then coding rounds, then more technical rounds. Not easy. I gave it everything.

Then the HR called.

Selected.

Good company. Good package. Good position. Three days in office, two from home.

I sat with it for a moment.

Remote job: work from anywhere. Laptop and WiFi. Travel India/World, work from different cities, explore in the evenings. The dream on paper.

Bangalore job: office exposure. Real engineering culture. Software engineering depth, which is still something I'm building. Mentorship I can't get through a screen.

I chose Bangalore.

Remote is for later. Right now I need to grow. The office will give me what remote can't.

I am good at AI/ML. Give me a problem, I will design a solution for it. But software engineering, I want to be better. More complete. And working in an office is how that happens.

Remote job is for later. Equip myself first.

---

The downside is real though.

Miss Family again.

Late night movies together, the four of us watching till 2am on weekends. Mom and me watching her singing shows on weekend evenings, tea and biscuits between us, me eventually dozing off on the sofa with one of the tortoises in my arms.

Toto is the male. Loves salad leaf. Directly walks into the kitchen, stretches out his neck, waves his little hand in our direction. I'm here. I'm hungry. Feed me now.

Tito is the female. Loves beans. If you put beans and something else in front of her, she ignores everything else and finds her beans. If you swap their food, they both just ignore it and go looking for what they actually want.

After eating, they find a corner of the house and sleep. Then come back and ask again. Tito does it by climbing on your feet and placing her hand there. Gentle. Patient. You're nearby, so feed me.

I will miss bathing them. Putting them under sunlight for half an hour while I play music and watch them move around in the water.

Therapy. That's the word.

My sister too. I'll miss our arguments that mean nothing and resolve nothing. She talks to me nicely mostly when she needs something done. Same applies to me, honestly.

Ahhhh life. You leave the people who make daily life worth living.

To build something bigger.

---

But this is the deal.

Energy: I have it.

Time: I have it.

Passion: more than I know what to do with.

Resources for learning: almost entirely free right now.

Best era in history to be an AI/ML engineer: yes, this one. Right now.

Go to Bangalore. Get the experience. Get deeper into the craft. And after enough time, a remote job. Or something of my own. Something that lets me live more fully. Travel. Come home whenever I want.

For now, Feb 9th is my joining date.

I've already started researching places to stay there.

Nervous, same as always.

Excited, same as always.

Ho ho! Here we go again.
